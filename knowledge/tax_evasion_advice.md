# TAX_EVASION_ADVICE
- **Source:** Tax Officer Advice Document (Updated 2026-06-12)
- **Core Message:** "Đừng trốn thuế - Chủ động kê khai - Nộp thuế"

## 1. Definitions of Tax Evasion (Trốn Thuế)
The following actions are considered tax evasion:
- **Failure to Register/Declare:** Not registering for tax, not declaring, or submitting declarations > 90 days late (unless taxes and late fees are paid before a tax audit decision/minutes are issued).
- **Accounting Failures:** Not recording revenues related to tax determination in accounting books.
- **Invoicing Failures:** Not issuing invoices or issuing invoices with values lower than the actual transaction value.
- **Illegal Documentation:** Using illegal invoices/documents to reduce tax or increase deductions.
- **Misrepresentation:** Using documents that do not reflect the actual nature/value of transactions.
- **Import/Export Fraud:** Wrongful declaration of goods or collusion to evade taxes.
- **Misuse of Exemptions:** Using tax-exempt goods for other purposes without reporting.

## 2. Legal Consequences
- **Criminal Liability:** Individuals may face criminal charges if the evaded tax amount is **100 million VND or more**.
- **Administrative Penalties:**
    - **Late Filing (Chậm nộp hồ sơ):** If the user voluntarily declares and pays full taxes + late fees before an audit, it is treated as "late filing" (approx. penalty range 10M VND for individuals in typical cases).
    - **Tax Evasion (Trốn thuế):** If caught before voluntary declaration, the penalty is typically **1.5 times the evaded tax amount**.

## 3. Example Calculation (2024-2025 Context)
For a seller with 4B revenue over 2 years (1.2B in 2024, 2.8B in 2025) who didn't declare:
- **Tax Threshold (2024-2025):** 100M/year.
- **GTGT (1%):** 4B x 1% = 40M VND.
- **TNCN (0.5%):** 4B x 0.5% = 20M VND (Note: Old regulation, no 1B threshold).
- **Business License Fee (Lệ phí môn bài):** ~2M VND for 2 years.
- **Late Fee:** 0.03% x days late x amount.

## 4. Key Advice for Users
- **Proactivity is Key:** Voluntarily declaring and paying taxes *before* an official audit converts a "Tax Evasion" (Criminal/Heavy Penalty) risk into a "Late Filing" (Administrative Penalty) risk.
- **Transparency:** Keep clean accounting books and issue invoices correctly.
